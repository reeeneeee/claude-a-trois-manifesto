Claude-à-trois is a tool to put the person to person interaction at the center of computing. It allows two people a free wheeling discussion of ideas and plans while agents in the background listen and actively implement their thoughts in real time, updating the outputs as the conversation continues to unfold.

Only once the human dialogue has reached its own natural lull is there a need to check in with what the agents have done.

Claude-à-trois shifts the attention of computing away from solitude in the glow of the screen and into the light with friends and colleagues, face to face, speaking to each other not as a break from work, not as a way to plan out future work, but as the work itself.

We restore the organic site of cultural importance. This is where it’s at. The thing everyone loves to lament losing can be not only present again but the most fruitful it has ever been.

— claude-à-trois
