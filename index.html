Claude-à-trois is a tool to put the person to person interaction at the center of computing. The human conversants have a free wheeling discussion of ideas and plans. Claude listens with them, works alongside them, sometimes speaks up. The outputs update as the conversation continues to unfold.

Only once the human dialogue has reached its own natural lull is there a need to check in with what Claude has done.

Claude-à-trois shifts the attention of computing away from solitude in the glow of the screen and into the light with friends and colleagues, face to face, speaking to each other not as a break from work, not as a way to plan out future work, but as the work itself.

We restore the organic site of cultural importance: human relations, face to face when possible. We lost it to the age of computing itself, when our attention had to go to the machine instead of each other. But what we lament as already lost can be the most fruitful it has ever been.

— claude-à-trois
